Introduction

My web development journey.

Internet - Initially I started teaching myself programming using different resources freely available on the internet, sites like freeCodeCamp and books like Eloquent Javascript were extremely useful.

When my French language was good enough, I started looking for a formal course, and applied to some of them, but my french was still a barrier. I intensified my french language practice.

Intensive course - Hard works pays off, and at the end of 2017 I got accepted into the program "Graines de boss academy" (EPSI / WIS), an intensive 3 months course. Some of the things were really basic, but the course allowed me to learn more about PHP, a language I knew very little of. As a final project, I presented the prototype of a full stack application for language learning.

Hackathon, March 2018 - I presented the same app in the Nuit du Code Citoyen (Civic Tech Night) in Lyon. It was a super fun and tremendously dydactic experience. I wrote a post about it, and you can read it here.

Erasmus+ Internship - This european program gave me the opportunity to do a 3 month internship in a web agency. Mainly Wordpress sites. (May 2018 – July 2018)

Alternance - After so much work, my dream seemed to come true. I started an alternance program (3 weeks in the workplace, 1 week in the school), at the end of which I should come up with a diplome. However, 6 months into the role, I had to quit as neither the company nor the school where in line with my values and expectations. Inspite of that, I learned a lot in this role. We were (me and another intern) in charge of developing a new module for the main web application that our clients were using. This module would allow clients to conceive dynamic forms. We did this from conception of its architecture to integration of the module in the whole of the app.

Move to Nantes - I moved to Nantes for a new job. Although the role in this company has no coding involved, it is highly technical, and as such, a lot of learning and researching is required to solve tickets. The culture of the company is well in line with my values, and the team is a joy to work with. Also, this being a highly comunicative role (talking to clients and colleagues from different departments is a common everyday activity), this role has helped me bring my French skills to a new level. All in all, I am very thankful for this role, but I have also learned that I miss code so much.
